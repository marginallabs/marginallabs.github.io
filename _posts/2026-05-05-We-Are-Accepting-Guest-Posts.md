Marginal Lab is now accepting guest contributions.

Whether you have taken an economics class, read a few books, or just followed economic ideas out of curiosity, if you have some exposure to economic thinking and an idea worth sharing, we would love to hear from you!

## What We Are Looking For

- **Topic** — Any economics topic is welcome
- **Format** — Posts should fit one of our three formats: [Essay]({{ '/blog/' | relative_url }}?format=essay), [Tutorial]({{ '/blog/' | relative_url }}?format=tutorial), or [Deep Dive]({{ '/blog/' | relative_url }}?format=deep-dive)
- **Language** — All posts must be written in English
- **Originality** — Posts must be original at the time of publication and not previously published elsewhere. After it goes live on Marginal Lab, you are welcome to cross-post it on your own site or elsewhere with a link back to the original
- **Flexibility** — No strict word count or formatting rules. We will work with you to get it publication-ready

## How It Works

We accept submissions in two ways:

### Option 1: Submit a Full Article

Already have a completed draft? Send it straight to us.

1. **Send your draft** — Email your complete article to [marginallab@proton.me](mailto:marginallab@proton.me) with the subject line **Guest Post: [Your Title]**. Include a short author bio and any links you would like included. Copy and paste the template below to get started:

```
Hi Marginal Lab,

I'd like to submit a guest post for consideration.

Title: [Your Title]
Format: Essay / Tutorial / Deep Dive
Brief summary: [summary of the post within 100 words]

[Attach your draft or paste it below]

Author name: [Your preferred name will be shown on the byline] 
Opt-in for author profile page: Yes / No
Author bio: [1–3 sentences about yourself]
Links: [Your website, social media, or academic profile]
```

2. **Review and revise** — We will review your draft and provide feedback, typically within 2 weeks. Most posts go through 1–2 rounds of revisions.
3. **Publish** — Once both sides are happy, we will publish it on Marginal Lab with full credit to you.

### Option 2: Pitch Your Idea

Prefer to check if your topic is a good fit before writing? Pitch it first.

1. **Pitch your idea** — Email a brief summary of your topic and which format you have in mind to [marginallab@proton.me](mailto:marginallab@proton.me) with the subject line **Guest Pitch: [Your Topic]**. We will respond within 1 week. Copy and paste the template below to get started:

```
Hi Marginal Lab,

I'd like to pitch a guest post idea.

Topic: [Your topic]
Format: Essay / Tutorial / Deep Dive
Brief summary: [summary of your plan, no word limit]

Author name: [Your preferred name will be shown on the byline] 
Opt-in for author profile page: Yes / No
Author bio: [1–3 sentences about yourself]
Links: [Your website, social media, or academic profile]
```

2. **Write your draft** — Once accepted, write at your own pace. We are happy to discuss structure or approach at any point along the way.
3. **Review and revise** — Send us your draft and we will provide feedback, typically within 2 weeks. Most posts go through 1–2 rounds of revisions.
4. **Publish** — Once both sides are happy, we will publish it on Marginal Lab with full credit to you.

## Author Credit

Every guest post is published with a byline. Optionally, we can create an author profile page for you with links to your website, social media, or academic profile.
