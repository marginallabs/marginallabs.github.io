**Marginal Lab** is a blog about economic thinking — not just what economists study, but how they reason.

The name comes from the concept of *thinking at the margin*: the idea that decisions are shaped not by totals, but by the next unit. Should you study one more hour? Should the government fund one more program? The answer always depends on what that *marginal* unit costs and what it delivers.

This blog applies that lens to economics, policy, and everyday decisions. Expect a mix of essays, tutorials, and the occasional deep dive into economic theory.

### About the author

An economics student based in Hong Kong, with interests in applied microeconomics, industrial organization, and causal inference. Uses R and Stata for empirical work. Cantonese native, writes in English.

If you think you know who writes this — you're probably right.
